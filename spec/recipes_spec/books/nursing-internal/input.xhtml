About The Nursing series

The Nursing series is is designed to support both semesters of the various nursing courses offered at both two-year and four-year institutions. Serving a student base of both majors and non-majors in the field, and an institutional variation in requirements of one or two semesters depending on the plan of study, the course introduces students to a in-depth study of nursing topics while focusing on helping students develop clinical judgment and critical thinking skills as well as preparing students for the new NGN.

Coverage and scope

Maternal Newborn Nursing: A Competency-Based Approach to Patient-Centered Care consists of 29 chapters ranging from both basic and core topics in the specialty, including current trends, and progresses to pregnancy, labor and birth, postpartum care, and finally, newborn care. The first unit is devoted to core topics in the specialty including foundations in maternal newborn nursing, women’s health, reproductive system, and current trends. The second unit then addresses pregnancy, including a chapter devoted to maternal and fetal assessments. The third unit provides comprehensive coverage of labor and delivery. The last two units cover postpartum care and newborn care. Each chapter focuses on nursing theory and critical thinking, with progress to clinical decision making as well as on collaboration with other nurses or health care professionals. Engaging feature boxes

Throughout the Nursing series, you will find features that engage students by taking selected topics a step further and challenge students to apply what they are learning. Each feature box contains either a link to a deeper exploration of the topic at hand or critical thinking questions that may be geared toward class discussion, student projects, or written essays. Our features include:

  • Making Connections.Unfolding Case Study. The Next Generation NCLEX (NGN) will measure nursing candidates’ clinical judgment ability using case-based scenarios. Unfolding case study features will follow one patient care story through a unit of the book. In the units that contain the case study, the first chapter will provide information from the initial patient encounter in the first module. As the content progresses in the chapters within the unit, the case study will unfold, offering more information about the patient with each box.
  • Real RN Stories. These vignettes should come from actual nurses who faced a problem the students are learning about in that section or chapter. They should not be fictional stories that read like real stories. Hearing a story about a topic makes it real for the learners and helps them unlock concepts and make connections in a way that mere descriptions cannot.
  • Life-stage and Cultural Contexts. These features will highlight age or cultural considerations as they relate to specific skills or topics. For example, age might affect the steps a nurse takes to bathe a neonatal patient as compared to an older child or adult. Cultural differences, such as skin tone or hair texture, may affect the way certain conditions or symptoms present or impact the steps a nurse takes. These boxes help students pay attention to these differences and explain how they may impact their approach to patient care.
  • Clinical Safety and Procedures (QSEN). The Clinical Safety and Procedure features will present students with step-by-step procedures in specific clinical settings to ensure proper protocols are followed and safety remains a focus. These features can come in many forms (often accompanied by useful diagrams or images) and with largely formulaic instructions for students on topics related to the surrounding textbook content.
  • Clinical Judgment Measurement Model. These features will focus on one of the six steps in the CJMM. These boxes help students make connections between nursing concepts and/or clinical findings and the steps of the CJMM.
  • Link to Learning. This feature includes a few sentences of introduction to a website with an interactive activity, animation, video, or primary source that helps improve student understanding.

Some feature boxes include questions for further exploration. These questions will encourage the student to apply critical thinking skills that prepare them for practice in healthcare.

Module materials that reinforce key concepts

Every module begins with a set of clear and concise learning objectives. These objectives are designed to help the instructor decide what content to include or assign, and to guide students on what they can expect to learn. After completing the module and end-of-module exercises, students should be able to demonstrate mastery of the learning objectives.

Section summaries distill the information in each module for both students and instructors down to key, concise points addressed in the section. Key terms are bold and are followed by a definition in context and at the end of each section for study. Additional important terms are glossed in an appendix glossary.

End-of-chapter materials to assess and extend student learning

Each chapter concludes with a series of assessments covering the content and learning objectives of each section.   •    Check Your Understanding Questions. Short-answer questions where students explain concepts in words.
  •    Reflection Questions. Short-answer assessments that ask students to reflect on concepts.
